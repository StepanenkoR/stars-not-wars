# What We Study

Stars Not Wars studies the direction of civilization.

This project is not only about war, and not only about the future. It is about the relationship between destruction and development, between short-term violent systems and long-term life-oriented growth.

We study how humanity uses its resources, what it chooses to invest in, what it destroys, what it builds, and what these choices say about the stage and direction of our civilization.

## Core areas of study

### 1. War and destructive systems
We study how much human energy, money, labor, attention, and political power are consumed by war, militarization, arms production, and adjacent destructive structures.

This includes:
- military expenditure
- war-related losses
- militarization trends
- the political logic of conflict and coercion

### 2. Life-oriented civilization indicators
We study the indicators that describe whether civilization is moving toward life, growth, and long-term development rather than fear, waste, and destruction.

This includes:
- life expectancy
- health and medicine
- scientific progress
- education
- research and development
- energy production and access
- technological capability
- economic capacity for long-term investment

### 3. Resource allocation
We study how civilization distributes its resources.

A central question of this project is simple:
How much is spent on death, and how much is spent on life?

We are interested in the comparison between:
- war and science
- militarization and medicine
- destruction and infrastructure
- short-term power games and long-term civilization building

### 4. Transition paths
We study what measurable transitions could move humanity away from war-centered systems and toward life-centered civilization.

This includes:
- redirection of resources
- better civilizational indicators
- energy abundance
- long-term scientific investment
- planetary coordination
- foundations for future space industry and stellar-scale development

### 5. The future horizon
We study not only what is wrong, but what becomes possible if humanity survives its destructive habits.

This includes questions about:
- long-term human development
- radical life extension
- planetary maturity
- post-war civilization models
- space industry
- Dyson Swarm and other large-scale future infrastructure

## What this project is not

Stars Not Wars is not:
- a party program
- a state ideology
- a propaganda machine
- a complete theory of politics
- a finished scientific institution

It is a research direction.

It is a place to collect data, frame questions, compare systems, and think seriously about how humanity can move from war, waste, and death toward life, growth, knowledge, and construction.

## In one sentence

Stars Not Wars studies how humanity spends its energy — on destruction or on the future.
