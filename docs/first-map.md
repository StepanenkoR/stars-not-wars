# First Map

This is the first structural map of Stars Not Wars.

It is not a final architecture.  
It is a working map that helps define what this project is, how its parts relate to each other, and where to move next.

## Project structure

Stars Not Wars has two main cores:

1. **Civilization Data Project**
2. **Open Research Lab**

These two cores support each other.

The data layer gives us measurable reality.  
The research layer gives us interpretation, comparison, questions, and direction.

Without data, the project becomes only a manifesto.  
Without research, the project becomes only a storage of numbers.

The goal is to build a place where facts and civilizational thinking reinforce each other.

---

## Layer 1 — Reality

This is the layer of observable civilization.

It includes the things humanity is already doing, funding, building, destroying, or neglecting.

This layer includes:
- war
- military spending
- economic production
- energy generation and consumption
- health and life expectancy
- science and R&D
- infrastructure
- education
- long-term capacity for growth

This is the layer of facts.

---

## Layer 2 — Indicators

This layer turns reality into measurable signals.

The purpose of indicators is not to collect everything, but to identify the few measures that reveal the direction of civilization.

Examples of core indicators:
- military expenditure
- military burden as % of GDP
- GDP
- energy use
- life expectancy
- R&D expenditure
- health expenditure
- scientific output
- education-related indicators

This is the layer where raw reality becomes structured evidence.

---

## Layer 3 — Comparisons

This layer asks the first serious civilizational question:

What is humanity choosing?

This layer compares:
- war vs science
- militarization vs health
- destruction vs infrastructure
- short-term power vs long-term growth
- death-oriented systems vs life-oriented systems

This is where numbers begin to form moral and strategic contrasts.

Not as propaganda, but as visible civilizational trade-offs.

---

## Layer 4 — Research Questions

This layer transforms data into inquiry.

It asks not only what is happening, but what it means, what patterns exist, and what alternative paths are possible.

The first research questions are:

1. How much of humanity’s resources are currently consumed by war, militarization, and adjacent destructive systems?
2. Which indicators best describe life-oriented civilization growth, and how do they compare to war-oriented resource allocation?
3. What measurable transition paths could move humanity from war-centered systems toward long-term life, planetary development, and eventually stellar-scale civilization?

This is where the project becomes a lab.

---

## Layer 5 — Civilizational Models

This layer begins to ask what a better direction would look like in measurable form.

Possible themes:
- life-oriented civilization
- long-term human development
- post-war civilizational priorities
- energy abundance
- planetary coordination
- radical life extension
- space industry
- Dyson Swarm as a long-horizon symbol and engineering direction

This layer is not about fantasy for its own sake.

It is about building conceptual models of a civilization that chooses life, growth, knowledge, and construction over fear, waste, and destruction.

---

## Layer 6 — Artifacts

This layer contains the things the project actually produces.

Examples:
- data files
- JSON libraries
- snapshots
- notes
- research documents
- comparisons
- visual diagrams
- scripts
- small models
- future simulations

Artifacts are important because they make the project real.

They are the first proof that the project is not only a set of emotions or ideas, but a growing body of work.

---

## What comes first

The first version of the project should stay small.

Version 0.1 should focus on:
- defining the project clearly
- framing the first research questions
- identifying the first core indicators
- creating the first small data artifacts
- writing the first short research notes

The first goal is not scale.

The first goal is coherence.

---

## In simple terms

Stars Not Wars works like this:

- observe civilization
- extract indicators
- compare resource directions
- ask research questions
- build models
- produce artifacts
- gradually form a clearer picture of what humanity is doing, what it could become, and what must change

This is the first map.
Not the last one.
